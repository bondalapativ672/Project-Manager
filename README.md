# 2. Revised Project Plan

## Current Situation

* Critical feature delayed by 10 days
* Marketing launch date remains fixed
* Design and engineering conflict causing blockers

## Revised Delivery Strategy

| Timeline | Focus Area               | Action                                                                    |
| -------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Week 1   | Scope Stabilization      | Freeze non-critical features and prioritize launch-critical functionality |
| Week 1   | Team Alignment           | Conduct alignment sessions between design and engineering teams           |
| Week 1   | Risk Management          | Begin daily risk reviews and blocker tracking                             |
| Week 2   | Testing & QA             | Prioritize testing for critical user journeys and production stability    |
| Week 2   | Stakeholder Coordination | Align launch messaging and delivery expectations                          |
| Week 3   | Phase 2 Release          | Deploy delayed feature after full validation and QA approval              |

## Trade-Off Decisions

### Features Shipping at Launch

* Core platform functionality
* Fully tested customer-facing workflows
* High-priority integrations

### Features Delayed

* Lower-priority enhancements
* Non-essential UI improvements
* Delayed feature moved to Phase 2 release

## Reasoning Behind the Decision

This approach protects launch commitments, maintains customer trust, and reduces the risk of production instability while still meeting key business objectives.

# 3. Conflict Resolution Approach

To address the growing conflict between design and engineering, I would first bring both teams into a focused alignment discussion.

My approach would include:

1. Understanding each team’s concerns objectively
2. Refocusing everyone on the shared launch goal
3. Separating technical limitations from design preferences
4. Prioritizing solutions based on business value and feasibility
5. Assigning clear ownership and action items

I would also establish short daily alignment checkpoints until blockers are resolved to ensure communication remains consistent and productive.

---

# 4. Prioritization Framework

I would prioritize work based on four key factors:

| Criteria         | Key Question                                                   |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Customer Impact  | Does this directly impact the customer experience?             |
| Business Impact  | Does this affect launch commitments, revenue, or partnerships? |
| Technical Risk   | Could this create production or stability issues?              |
| Time Sensitivity | Is this essential for launch day success?                      |

## Priority Levels

* P1: Must ship for launch
* P2: Important but can move to Phase 2
* P3: Nice-to-have improvements

This framework helps keep decisions objective, business-focused, and aligned across teams.

## Communication Plan

* Twice-weekly stakeholder updates
* Immediate escalation path for unresolved blockers
* Transparent reporting on launch readiness and risks

## Engineering Controls

* Temporary feature freeze on non-critical work
* Additional QA cycles for launch-critical functionality

## Team Management

* Resolve blockers quickly through direct coordination
* Reduce unnecessary meetings to maintain execution focus
* Keep teams aligned around launch priorities

# 6. Bonus Challenge Response

## Handling the Politically Sensitive Situation

I would handle the situation professionally by focusing first on stabilizing delivery and ensuring all stakeholders now have accurate visibility into project risks and impacts. Rather than assigning blame publicly, I would document the current status clearly, communicate realistic expectations, and work collaboratively with leadership to move the project forward successfully.

Once the immediate launch concerns are stabilized, I would address the missed escalation privately with leadership and discuss how earlier transparency could have reduced delivery risk and team pressure.

## Systemic Improvement Recommendation

Implement a mandatory weekly risk escalation review where all high-impact technical risks must be documented, reviewed, and acknowledged by leadership and project stakeholders. This creates accountability, improves transparency, and prevents critical risks from remaining hidden.

# 7. Process & Decision-Making Explanation

## How I Approached the Scenario

My primary focus was balancing business commitments with product stability and customer experience.

The first step was understanding the impact of the engineering delay and identifying whether the launch date could still be partially preserved through a phased delivery approach.

Next, I prioritized communication and transparency to ensure stakeholders received clear updates early instead of being surprised later in the process.

At the same time, I addressed the team conflict because unresolved communication issues between design and engineering can create additional delivery delays.

Finally, I used a structured prioritization framework to determine which features were essential for launch and which could safely move into a later release without creating major business impact.

## Why This Approach Works

This strategy:

* Protects customer experience
* Reduces production risk
* Maintains stakeholder trust
* Keeps teams aligned during pressure situations
* Allows the business to preserve important launch commitments while maintaining delivery quality
